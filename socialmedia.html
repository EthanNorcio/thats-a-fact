Social Media Platforms - That's A Fact!

Nowadays, social media platforms almost always provide the most up-to-date information. This content not only includes the news-worthy, but also the events in the daily lives of your close friends.

Social media may be the fastest platform to spread information, but since everyone can contribute to these websites, the authenticity news we get from these platforms may be questionable.

Here are some examples of social media platforms that may provide questionable information.

  • Famous in the Philippines: Facebook, Tiktok, and YouTube
  • Other Commonly Used Social Media Platforms: Twitter, Instagram, and Reddit

Usually, social media platforms have "pages" which will show similar content that share a common topic. They also have "account pages" which can be used to view all the content created or shared by a certain account. Learning how to use them will let you know where to find the information you need quickly.

Here are the usual account pages that probably contain true and reliable information.

  • Pages controlled by News Networks
  • Pages controlled by Expert Organizations
    • For example, the Facebook page of DOST-PAGASA contains reliable news and information about the weather such as Typhoon Cyclone Wind Signals during a typhoon.
  • Verified Accounts*

We can expect news networks to be reliable sources of information since they are trusted to report day-to-day events accurately and timely. We need to be careful though, since some posers may try to replicate them.
Expert Organizations are considered reliable since they have enough knowledge to report on a certain specific field.
Verified accounts are social media users who are celebrities, government officials, or generally famous people. It's difficult to differentiate true information than personal opinions so fact-checking is very important.
