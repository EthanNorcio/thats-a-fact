Sharing Information - That's A Fact!

We share information on social media platforms for our close friends to know about it too.
In some social media websites, we can contribute content to show in the "News Feed" or "Homepage" of our friends.

We do not want to be abusing this power ourselves.
We need to make sure that the information we share is factual, timely, and not misleading.

Here are some questions to answer before sharing information online.

  • Does the article contain only true information?
  • Is the article timely or is it relevant to a certain timely event?
  • Try reading the article again. Does it deliver the message you want to convey?

If at least one of your answers to the previous questions is NO, then you need to either revise your message, add a disclaimer or a clarification, or just don't proceed at all. This is important to make sure that you are not spreading fake news, and that the receiver of your message understands what you are trying to say.
In formal or public discussions, make sure to cite your references properly.



Video Source: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_fVv2Jt0o18

Citing your references is important in formal and public discussions if necessary to make you more credible, and also to make sure that you are not committing plagiarism.

The video on the right from the YouTube channel CSUDH Library (2019) explains how to use the APA 7th Edition to cite different references.

Aside from protecting yourself from plagiarism, you also help the readers or viewers of your content by letting them know where you got the data from since your content is probably just a secondary source.

The author of your primary source probably have conducted extensive research or other types of preparation depending on the context such as surveying or experimenting. It's not right to steal and claim their work.
