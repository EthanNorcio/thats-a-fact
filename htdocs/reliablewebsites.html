Reliable Websites - That's A Fact!

Now that you know why fact-checking is important, let's go over some websites that actually give correct information.

Here are some categories of websites that produce reliable news.

  • News Sites (Examples: ABS-CBN, Rappler, and GMA)
  • Expert Organizations (Examples: WHO, and CDC)
  • Government Agencies (Examples: DOH, DOST, and DepEd)

We know that news sites give correct information because they report immediately after an event happens or they quote experts who know what they are talking about. In case a news site gives wrong information, it will usually give an apology or erratum since their report will contradict other news channels.
Experts and government agencies are also reliable because they know the information first-hand. They usually know the information before anyone else does, and most news articles have them as sources.

Here are some websites that contain unreliable information.

  • A Random Account on Social Media not in the list of Reliable Sources
  • Personal Pages and Websites
  • Wikipedia and other Wiki Websites
  • Unverfied Answers on Peer Learning Websites (Examples: Brainly, and Stack Overflow)

Random Accounts on Social Media are the most common spreaders of fake information. Virtually anyone can make an account in sits like Facebook, YouTube, or Twitter and they can already spread unverified content.
Personal Websites are also considered unreliable. This is because the information present in these sites may be influenced by the personal opinion of the author.
Wikipedia sites and some answers on peer learning websites can also be unreliable since anyone can contribute to these websites. However, you can still get significant information in these sites. See the page "What About Wikis?"
